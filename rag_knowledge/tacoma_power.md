# Tacoma Power Project

## Summary
The Tacoma Power project analyzed 10+ years of wood pole inspection data to identify the main drivers of pole replacement and turn historical inspection records into actionable business insights.

## Key Facts
- Project type: Machine learning / data science capstone
- Organization: Tacoma Power
- Main model: XGBoost
- Main business question: What factors are driving pole replacement?
- Main finding: Pole top condition was the dominant driver of pole replacement
- 2025 holdout recall: 99.6%
- Pole top deterioration accounted for roughly 43% of replacements

## My Role
- Cleaned and standardized historical inspection data
- Helped transform messy Excel records into usable analytical datasets
- Built machine learning models to predict pole rejection
- Used SHAP/model interpretability to identify replacement drivers
- Helped translate technical findings into business recommendations

## Technologies
- Python
- pandas
- XGBoost
- SHAP
- Snowflake
- Tableau/Streamlit
- Excel data ingestion

## Important Details
- The project involved over a decade of inspection data
- The analysis found that pole top condition was the strongest driver of replacement
- A business example estimated that reducing pole replacements by 5% could save approximately $420,000 before treatment costs, assuming 840 replacements per year and $10,000 per replacement
- The main recommendation was to prioritize pole top preservation because it had the highest business value
